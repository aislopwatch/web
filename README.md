# AISlopWatch

**AI is fine. What people do with it often isn't.**

AISlopWatch is a logbook of two kinds of AI cringe — screenshotted, labeled, and
blamed. We're **not anti-AI**: AI is a fine tool. We're anti-_lazy_ and
anti-_grift_, and we collect the receipts.

🔗 **[aislopwatch.com](https://aislopwatch.com)**

## The two crimes

- **Slop** — low-effort, machine-extruded filler that nobody read back before
  shipping. The "Top 10" listicle that's three sentences in a trench coat; the
  blender review written by something that has never owned hands.
- **Hype** — wide-awake humans swearing a chatbot loves them, will replace your
  job by Tuesday, or made them $40k with a single prompt. Engagement is the
  product and the receipts are thin.

Bonus points when a post is both: AI-written cringe about how important AI is.

## How it works

- **Browse** the sightings — newest first, or sort by *most-blamed* /
  *most-viewed*, and filter by verdict (slop · hype · guide · tell · meta).
- **Blame it** — hit 🔨 *blame this slop* on any post. One blame per visitor,
  cookieless, no account needed. The most-blamed bubble up to the
  **[Hall of Shame](https://aislopwatch.com/trending/)**.
- **Comment** — sign in with GitHub and weigh in at the bottom of any post.
- **Submit a sighting** — caught some slop in the wild? Open a sighting (see
  below). The community votes 👍 / 👎; winners get published automatically, and
  you get the credit.

## Submit a sighting

Spotted slop or hype that deserves the desk? Screenshot it before they quietly
delete it, then **[submit a sighting →](https://github.com/aislopwatch/web/issues/new?template=slop-sighting.yml)**.

Note where you found it, what gave it away, and who profits. The receipts are
the whole point — anyone can say "this is slop"; we prefer exhibits. A sighting
gets published when it clears the community vote **or** an editor approves it.

> If a human would be embarrassed to have written it, and no human will admit to
> having written it, it is probably slop.

## No trackers, no slop

System fonts only. No webfonts, no third-party scripts, no cookies, no analytics
that watch *you*. The only numbers we keep are **aggregate** blames and views —
we count the slop, not the visitor. That footer line is literally true.
