## What is this

**We are not anti-AI. We are anti-_lazy_ and anti-_grift_.** AI is a fine tool —
the cheap, low-effort junk people ship with it is the problem. AISlopWatch
screenshots the internet's worst AI _misuse_ and holds it up to the light.
There is a difference between using a tool well and either bolting a firehose to
the open web or selling a $499 course about the firehose.

It arrives in two flavors, and we collect both.

First, **slop**: low-effort, machine-extruded text and images, shipped because
they are cheap, not because they are good. The "Top 10" listicle that is three
sentences in a trench coat. The product review for a blender, written by
something that has never owned a blender, a kitchen, or hands — and posted by
someone who never read it.

Second, **the hype**: humans — very much awake, very much typing — announcing
that AI is now sentient, about to make them rich, and the single most
important thing you must adopt immediately or perish. The slop didn't read
itself. The hype guy read it twice and called it a revelation.

## What counts

A post earns a place here when it is one of these:

- **Slop** — nobody who shipped it read it back to themselves once; it exists
  to farm a ranking or fill space, and you can tell.
- **Hype** — a confident human announcing that a chatbot loves them, will
  replace your job by Tuesday, or made them $40k with a single prompt;
  engagement is the product and the receipts are thin.

Bonus points when a post is both: AI-written cringe about how important AI is.

## How to contribute a sighting

Spotted one in the wild? Screenshot it before they quietly delete it. Note
where you found it, what gave it away, and who profits from the post. The
receipts are the whole point — anyone can say "this is slop," we prefer
exhibits.

## "Wait, was this site built with AI?"

Short answer: yes. Are we ashamed? Sort of. A solid "kind of."

And that is the whole point, so stay with us. As we said up top, we were never
anti-AI. We used a model to help build this thing, the same way you'd reach for
a power drill instead of gnawing through the wall. A tool, doing a job it is
good at, with a human who read the output, tested it, and stands behind it.

That last part is the entire difference. AI has its place. It writes the
boilerplate, it drafts, it suggests, it handles the boring 80%. What it cannot
do is care. The problem was never that a machine was involved. The problem is
shipping the machine's first draft as if a person made it, with nobody in the
loop who actually looked. Using a tool well and bolting a firehose to the open
web are not the same act, even when they share a tool.

Everyone has that one aunt who bought a laser engraver. One week she is curious,
the next the whole family owns engraved coasters, a slate plaque that reads
"Live Laugh Laser", and a keychain shaped like the dog. Nobody asked for any of
it. Nobody can throw it out either, because she is right there beaming. AI is
that aunt, except the plywood never runs out and the entire internet is her
fridge door. The laser, at least, has to stop and cool down.

So no, the irony is not lost on us. We just do not think "made with AI" is the
insult. "Made with AI, unread, unloved, and shoved online to farm a ranking" is
the insult. We aim to be the first kind. The day we become the second, screenshot
us. Fair is fair.
